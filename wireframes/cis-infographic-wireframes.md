# CIS Infographic Website Wireframes

## Goal

Create a one-page, mobile-first scrollytelling infographic site for the **Community Impact Report 2025-26**.

The wireframe supports three jobs:

1. Let community members understand the findings in under five minutes.
2. Give donors, leaders, and institutions a clear picture of priorities, trust, and perceived impact.
3. Drive action: join the next study, submit questions, support/collaborate, read methodology, and download/share the report.

## Data modules

### Hero stats

- 1,400+ respondents
- 92.5% feel more concerned and less safe
- 90.2% say the community was not well prepared
- Major Jewish centres across Canada

### Priority ladder

1. Safety
2. Antisemitism
3. Youth engagement
4. Community mobilization
5. Training & education
6. Advocacy & lobbying
7. PR / communications
8. Policy & research

### Lived experience

- Preparedness: 90.2% disagree community was well prepared.
- Safety: 92.5% agree they feel more concerned and less safe.

### Identity pressure

- Public identity without fear: ~63.0% disagree, ~18.8% agree.
- Symbols likely targeted: 84.6% agree.
- Enhanced security for recognizable Jewish individuals/institutions: 93.6% agree.
- Immediate security improvements: 89.6% agree.

### Institutional satisfaction

Show respondent satisfaction for B’nai Brith Canada, FSWC, AGPI, JSN, CJPAC, CIJA, and UJA Federation with neutral language and a “not an audit” note.

### Grassroots vs legacy

Use weighted score bars:

- Policy & legislation: 29.7% grassroots / 70.3% legacy
- Media coverage: 41.0% grassroots / 59.0% legacy
- Community safety: 47.5% grassroots / 52.5% legacy
- Education-system support: 52.6% grassroots / 47.4% legacy
- Community mobilization: 57.1% grassroots / 42.9% legacy
- Public opinion: 58.9% grassroots / 41.1% legacy
- Unity & belonging: 67.3% grassroots / 32.7% legacy

## Visual style

- Deep navy background sections for authority.
- Warm off-white surface for readability.
- Gold accents for LOJ / report identity.
- Coral/red for threat and risk.
- Green for action, improvement, and grassroots energy.
- Blue for national data, map, and institutional comparison.

---

## Accessibility notes

- All charts need text values visible, not color-only.
- Add source/caveat notes to sensitive charts.
- Provide readable data cards instead of tiny chart labels on mobile.
- Respect reduced motion.
- Keep named-institution comparisons neutral and contextualized.
